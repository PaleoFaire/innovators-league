## Why these matter

These are companies where a solo $100-250K check probably won't land a seat in the lead round. But they're too good to ignore — five alternate paths:

1. **SPV participation** — ride the lead's check via a special-purpose vehicle. Common for Series B/C, even when primary is closed.
2. **LP into an existing-lead's fund** — Lux, Founders Fund, Khosla, Sequoia, a16z American Dynamism all take small LPs in exchange for co-invest rights in their deals.
3. **Secondary market** — Forge, EquityZen, Setter or direct founder-secondary transactions. $250K is plenty to open a position in a $2B company at a 10-20% discount.
4. **Content-for-access** — interview the founder for ROS Substack/podcast. Reciprocal: you get to know them + signal commitment; they get distribution. Then when a round opens, you're on the call sheet.
5. **Future-round access** — companies raising $100-300M today will raise $300-700M+ in 18 months. Relationship compounds.

## How to work this list

This is not a list of 'email them and ask for allocation.' These are **relationships to build over 6-24 months**:

1. **Email the active raisers first** — any Form D filer gets a same-week follow-up with a specific SPV/secondary ask.
2. **For the top 10-15 that match your thesis best** — pitch a ROS Substack/podcast feature. Stephen's distribution is a real asset; founders of $1B+ companies want the megaphone.
3. **For defense/space names specifically** — map which primes (a16z American Dynamism, Lux, 8VC, Founders Fund, Thrive) led each round. LP-ing $1M into one of those funds gets co-invest rights across multiple portfolio companies at once.
4. **For founders-you-haven't-met on the list** — warm intros via Founder DNA network. The COMPANIES database tracks who's an ex-SpaceX / ex-Anduril / Palantir mafia; 2-3 founders away from anyone on the list.
5. **Re-run quarterly** — `python3 scripts/calc_ros_stretch_top50.py`. As companies grow out of the $100-500M band (into $500M+) they drop; as others grow into it from seed tier, they enter.
