# The defense transformation is past the point of no return.

For sixty years, the US defense industrial base worked on cost-plus cycles, 20-year development programs, and a fixed roster of five primes. That system is now structurally broken, and the break is visible in the data on this page.

The clearest signal isn't the VC dollars — it's the **contracts**. Anduril's $642M USMC Counter-UAS program of record. Saronic's $392M Corsair production deal closed in under 12 months from prototype. Palantir beating RTX on TITAN. Epirus pulling IFPC-HPM out of incumbent hands. These are program-of-record awards, not one-off SBIR experiments. They mean the Pentagon is now treating frontier companies as primes.

**What's different this time:**

1. **Software flipped first, and it pulled hardware with it.** Palantir won against Raytheon on the most advanced Army targeting system. That was the signal that the cultural argument was over. Hardware companies like Anduril, Saronic, and Shield AI then inherited the credibility dividend.

2. **Replicator is the policy unlock.** The Pentagon has an explicit target of thousands of attritable autonomous systems in 18-24 months. Every named Replicator beneficiary is a frontier company, not a prime.

3. **R&D math is inverted.** The three tracked incumbents spent roughly $7B of company-funded R&D in 2024. The frontier primes are deploying 3-4x that in VC every 24 months — and they spend it on product, not on cost-plus overhead.

**What I'm watching:** the first big loss. We haven't yet seen a frontier prime miss a delivery, blow a safety gate, or have a $500M+ program canceled. When that happens (it will), the incumbent lobbying machine will weaponize it. The frontier needs to keep its discipline sharp through that moment — and investors need to underwrite companies with real manufacturing capacity, not just software margins.

**Portfolio stance:** long Anduril, Shield AI, Saronic, Epirus, Castelion, Neros. Cautious on any frontier defense company that hasn't yet won a program of record by mid-2027. The window for startup-to-prime transitions is wide open today; it will not be in three years.

— Stephen McBride, Rational Optimist Society
